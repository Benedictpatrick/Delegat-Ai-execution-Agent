## Positioning & Core Message

**The Problem**: Productivity tools are passive. They are digital filing cabinets that wait for you to do the work. When you fall behind, they just turn red and make you feel guilty.

**The Solution**: Delegat is an active participant. It is an **AI Execution Agent**.

### Core Messaging Pillars

1. **"We do the scaffolding. You do the thinking."**
   - *Message*: Stop staring at blank pages. Delegat creates the doc, formats the sections, drafts the email, and books the time. You just fill in the brilliance.
2. **"The only tool that helps you recover."**
   - *Message*: Todo lists punish you for falling behind. Delegat's Recovery Engine detects when you're slipping and actively re-plans your day to get you back on track.
3. **"Command Center, not a Checklist."**
   - *Message*: The War Room provides real-time situational awareness (Health Scores, Risk Radar), designed for execution, not just storage.

### Elevator Pitch
*"Delegat is an AI Execution Agent that doesn't just remind you about your work—it autonomously breaks it down, drafts your emails, creates your documents, and actively manages your schedule to ensure you never miss a deadline."*

## Target Audience

### Primary: The Overwhelmed Professional (Founders, PMs, Consultants)
- **Pain Point**: Context switching. 50% of their day is spent setting up the work (creating docs, finding calendar slots, drafting status emails) rather than doing the work.
- **Value**: Agent 3 (Execution) saves them 1-2 hours a day.

### Secondary: The Neurodivergent Knowledge Worker (ADHD)
- **Pain Point**: Executive dysfunction and activation energy. Breaking a large project into steps is paralyzing. Falling behind creates a shame spiral.
- **Value**: Agent 2 (Decomposition) removes the cognitive load of planning. Agent 4 (Recovery Engine) provides micro-nudges to break paralysis.

### Tertiary: Students (University / Grad)
- **Pain Point**: Juggling multiple hard deadlines with poor time management skills.
- **Value**: The Timeline and Health Score keep them accountable before the night before the deadline.

## Acquisition Channels

### 1. Organic Social & "Build in Public" (X/Twitter & LinkedIn)
- Share the technical challenges of building multi-agent systems.
- Share visual snippets of the War Room (the UI is highly distinctive and dramatic, making for good visual content).
- Target the tech/startup community.

### 2. SEO & Programmatic Content
- Create landing pages targeting specific "How to plan X" queries.
- E.g., "How to plan a product launch", "How to write a research paper".
- Delegat acts as the dynamic solution to these static queries.

### 3. Community / Niche Groups
- Productivity subreddits (`r/productivity`, `r/ADHD`).
- Position the tool carefully: not as "another todo app," but as an "execution engine."

## Content Strategy

### The "Execution vs. Planning" Narrative
Content should consistently attack the premise that "better planning" solves productivity issues. 
- *Article*: "Why your Notion setup is actually a procrastination tool."
- *Article*: "The Activation Energy Crisis in Knowledge Work."
- *Article*: "How we built an AI that catches you when you fall behind."

## Launch Plan

### Phase 1: Private Beta (Months 1-2)
- Focus: Polish the Agent 3 execution reliability (ensure Gmail drafts and Docs creation are flawless).
- Audience: 100-200 highly engaged users from existing networks.
- Goal: Validate the Health Score accuracy and refine the Recovery Engine triggers.

### Phase 2: Product Hunt Launch (Month 3)
- Assets required: High-quality walkthrough video showing the "magic moment" (typing a vague sentence and watching Delegat instantly generate tasks, a calendar block, and a drafted email).
- Offer: "Pro Tier free for 3 months" to drive immediate adoption.

### Phase 3: SEO Scaling (Month 4+)
- Deploy programmatic landing pages.
- Begin performance marketing (Google Ads) targeting high-intent keywords ("AI task automation", "AI calendar manager").
